B-free Manifest (1999.12.23)

  1. B-Free Project

    2. B-Free project is a collaborative work aimed at creating a BTRON operating system from scratch, with free distribution, which is called "B-Free operating system."

    B-Free operating system is supposed to be a preemptive multitask operating system constructed through micro-kernel technology, which assumes 32-bit or higher CPUs.

    Three encouraging facts help assure that such a big undertaking can be completed successfully even by the gathering of personal-level works:

    • There are many ITRON operating systems implemented by individuals.
    • There are BTRON operating systems implemented upon ITRON kernels.
    • Linux, a UNIX clone, was made by Linus B.Torvalds who was a Finish college student, and a number of its copies are now in operation over the world.

  2. Characteristics of B-Free Project

    • Publication of the outcomes

      • All outcomes of B-Free project will be made public, and also they must be freely redistributed under the term of GNU General Public License (GPL).

    • Copyright

      • Copyrights occurring in B-Free project belong to B-Free project itself and the members who own them.

    • Membership

      • Anyone can participate in the B-Free project at any time by finishing some part of the tasks in the project.

  3. The Goal

    4. We intend the following fruits of B-Free project, as it progresses:

    • A BTRON-spec. operating system for 32-bit or higher CPUs.
    • Its self-development environment.
    • Its applications.
    • Various kinds of documentation.

    All of the above, even their source codes, are to follow GNU General Public License (GPL).
